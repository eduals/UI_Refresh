BSc (Honours) Natural Sciences

Course overview Course details Am I ready? Fees & funding Careers

Course overview

We live in a society that depends on science, but how many of us really understand the science behind the important issues that feature in the news? Whether you’re keen to study a specific area of science, or you’re not yet sure where your interests lie – say in physics or biology – this degree is for you. 

Key features of the course

  • Starts with a wide-ranging introduction to highly topical areas of modern science, giving you a good grounding in each
  • Carry on with a broad-based approach, or focus on a particular area – choosing from astronomy and planetary science, biology, chemistry, Earth sciences, environmental sciences or physics 
  • Opt for hands-on laboratory and field work, or take part online from home
  • Build valuable transferable skills such as analytical, numerical and communication skills, team working, problem solving and IT.

Course details

This degree has three stages, each comprising 120 credits (equivalent to one year's full-time university study). Stage 1 provides the underpinning knowledge and skills needed for more advanced study at Stages 2 and 3.

Stage 1 (120 credits)

Everyone starts with a broad and absorbing introduction to study of science. You’ll investigate the major scientific issues affecting contemporary society, and develop important scientific concepts and skills to give you a solid foundation for higher level study. You'll then be introduced to practical science, before completing Stage 1 with an optional module.

Compulsory modules (90 credits)

Managing in the workplace (B121)

This module about management skills and ideas is ideal if you’ve completed NVQ Level 3 in management – or similar – and want to begin undergraduate study.

Investigative and mathematical skills in science (S141)

With this module you’ll develop your experimental, investigative and mathematical skills as well gaining a better understanding of the weather and how it is forecast.

Optional modules (30 credits)

Select one of:

  • Introducing health sciences: a case study approach (SDK125)
  • Topics in science (S142)
  • Topics in health sciences (SK143)
  • Essential mathematics 1 (MST124)
  • Or 30 credits from any other OU level 1 module

Decision to make

Your choice of optional module will be affected by your route through this degree. You must study Essential mathematics 1 (MST124) if you want to choose physics or astronomy and planetary science at Stages 2 and 3.

Stages 2 and 3 (240 credits)

You can choose to continue with a broad-based study of science, or to focus your studies on one of:

  • Biology – the structure and function of cells, the importance of cell biology in human health and technology, how evolutionary adaptation contributes to the survival of whole organisms 
  • Chemistry – the essential concepts of chemistry underpinning the development of everyday materials, medicines and microchips 
  • Earth sciences – the Earth’s structure; volcanoes, earthquakes and plate tectonics; the structure and geological history of rocks and minerals
  • Environmental sciences – holistic study of Earth, air, water and life through topics such as global warming, water resources, pollution and conservation 
  • Physics – energy, the behaviour of light, radioactive decay and Universal processes
  • Astronomy and planetary science – the stars and their life cycles, galaxies and quasars, planetary processes, the origin of the Universe, and evidence for extra-terrestrial life.

The modules quoted in this description are currently available for study. However, as we review the curriculum on a regular basis, the exact selection may change over time.
