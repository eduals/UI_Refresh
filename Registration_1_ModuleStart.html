Undergraduate Study

The levels indicate the complexity or difficulty of our modules. They are progressive:.

  • Level 1 is the usual starting point
  • Level 2 builds on study at Level 1
  • Level 3 builds on study at Level 2

Unless you've studied your chosen subject at university level before, or have professional experience, we strongly recommend that you start with a Level 1 module.

Postgraduate Study

If you have chosen a postgraduate module you should be aware that many of these modules have enrolment requirements. Please ensure that you have read the module description to find out if you meet these requirements before you proceed.

For modules with enrolment requirements, a supplementary information form is available at the end of the registration pathway for you to complete and return.

For more advice about choosing the level that's right for you, go to our advice on choosing the right module.

Time

An OU qualification is built up gradually by earning credit for each module. The amount of time it takes to complete a qualification is largely up to you. You can study quickly, slowly, vary the pace, or even have a break between modules. However, a few qualifications - where module content may date â€“ have to be completed within a time limit.

For each chosen module, you'll need to plan ahead and consider:

  • how long the module lasts
  • how many hours of study you need to do each week

Information on the average time it takes to complete different sorts of qualifications and modules can be found on the How much time does it take? pages

Resources

Most study materials are provided as part of the module, but look at the module description to see whether you'll have to:

  • buy set books
  • arrange access to a computer and the internet
  • attend compulsory tutorials, day or residential schools

If you have a disability, find out about the services and equipment we can offer to help your study.

To find out more about study materials and computing requirements go to what are the study materials like?

Payment options

There are several ways to pay. For advice about them and about financial support, go to the how to pay pages.

English language requirements

Are you worried about whether you can produce a good enough standard of academic English for your studies?

The English for learning pages provide support and guidance on improving your English skills and will help you discover how academic English is used in our modules.

Country of residence

In order to gain an Open University qualification you will need to study a number of specified modules. If you live outside the UK and Agreed Study Areas, you may find that not all the modules required for successful completion of your chosen qualification are available. Visit the Where Can I Study pages to check whether the modules you want to study are presented where you live.

Agreed Study Areas:

Aland Islands, Andorra, Austria, Belgium, Bulgaria, Channel Islands, Cyprus, Czech Republic, Denmark, Estonia, Finland, France, Germany, Gibraltar, Greece, Hungary, Isle of Man, Italy, Latvia, Liechtenstein, Lithuania, Luxembourg, Malta, Monaco, Netherlands, Poland, Portugal, Republic of Ireland, Romania, San Marino, Slovakia, Slovenia, Spain, Sweden, Switzerland and Vatican City State
